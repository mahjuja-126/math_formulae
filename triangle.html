Triangle Theorems

Vertically Opposite Angles

When two straight lines intersect, vertically opposite angles are equal.
Example: If lines AB and CD intersect at O:
   ∠AOC = ∠BOD
   ∠COB = ∠AOD

Angles Formed by Parallel Lines and a Transversal

  • Corresponding angles are equal (e.g., ∠PEB = ∠EFD)
  • Alternate interior angles are equal (e.g., ∠AEF = ∠EFD)
  • Interior angles on the same side of the transversal are supplementary (sum = 180°)

Condition for Two Lines to be Parallel

Two lines are parallel if:
a) Corresponding angles are equal
b) Alternate interior angles are equal
c) Interior angles on the same side of the transversal sum to 180°

Sum of Angles in a Triangle

∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°

Exterior Angle of a Triangle

Exterior angle = sum of two opposite interior angles
Property: Exterior angle > each of its interior opposite angles

Right-Angled Triangle

Acute angles are complementary: sum = 90°

Pythagorean Theorem:
AC² = AB² + BC² (for right triangle ABC with ∠B = 90°)

Triangle Inequalities

Difference of any two sides < third side
Example: |AB - AC| < BC

Isosceles Triangle Properties

  • Angles opposite equal sides are equal (AB = AC → ∠B = ∠C)
  • Sides opposite equal angles are equal (∠B = ∠C → AB = AC)

Congruence of Triangles

  • SSS (Side-Side-Side): Three sides equal → triangles congruent
  • ASA (Angle-Side-Angle): Two angles and included side equal → triangles congruent
  • RHS (Right angle-Hypotenuse-Side): Hypotenuse and one side equal → triangles congruent

Midpoint Theorem

Line joining midpoints of two sides is parallel to the third side and half its length.

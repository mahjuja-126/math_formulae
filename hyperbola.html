Hyperbola (x²/a² − y²/b² = 1)

Standard Diagram

Diagram showing center, vertices, foci, asymptotes, axes, and latus rectum.

Definition

A hyperbola is the locus of a point such that the absolute difference of its distances from two fixed points (foci) is constant.

Standard Properties

  • Standard Equation: x²/a² − y²/b² = 1
  • Center: (0, 0)
  • Eccentricity: e = √((a² + b²) / a²)
  • Vertices: (±a, 0)
  • Foci: (±ae, 0)
  • Directrices: x = ±a / e

Axes

  • Transverse Axis: y = 0
  • Length of Transverse Axis: 2a
  • Conjugate Axis: x = 0
  • Length of Conjugate Axis: 2b

Latus Rectum

  • Length of Latus Rectum: 2b² / a

Asymptotes

y = ±(b/a)x

Parametric Coordinates

A point on the hyperbola can be expressed parametrically.

(x, y) = (a secθ, b tanθ)

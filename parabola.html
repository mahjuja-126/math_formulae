Parabola (y² = 4ax)

Standard Diagram

Diagram of parabola showing vertex, focus, directrix, axis, and latus rectum.

Definition

A parabola is the locus of a point whose distance from a fixed point (focus) is equal to its perpendicular distance from a fixed line (directrix).

SP = PM

Basic Properties

  • Standard Equation: y² = 4ax
  • Eccentricity (e): 1
  • Vertex: (0, 0)
  • Focus: (a, 0)
  • Axis of Parabola: y = 0
  • Directrix: x = −a

Latus Rectum

  • Equation of Latus Rectum: x = a
  • Length of Latus Rectum: 4a
  • Endpoints: (a, 2a), (a, −2a)

Distance Properties

  • Distance from P(x, y) to Focus = √[(x − a)² + y²]
  • Distance from P(x, y) to Directrix = |x + a|

Parametric Coordinates

Any point on the parabola can be represented parametrically.

(x, y) = (at², 2at)

Equation of Tangent

Tangent to the parabola at point (x₁, y₁).

yy₁ = 2a(x + x₁)

Shifted Parabola

Parabola with vertex at (α, β).

(y − β)² = 4a(x − α)
